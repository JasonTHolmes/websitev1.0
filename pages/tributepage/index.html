Sherlock Holmes

The world's first Consulting Detective
  • January 6th, 1854 - Born in England
  • 1873 - Sherlock Enters University - unable to make friends due to his distinct interests, Sherlock becomes skilled in Fencing and boxing as well as maintaining his acedemic studies
  • Summer, 1874 - Sherlock solves his first case
  • 1875 - Sherlock leaves University
  • 1877 - At the age of 23, Sherlock begins his career as the world's first Consulting Detective where he starts his practice on Montegue Street in London.
  • Summer, 1779 - Winter, 1880 - As Sherlocks practice grows, Sherlock takes on higher profile clients and through successful cases, grows his connections at an exorbitant rate
  • January 1st-2nd 1881 - Sherlock meets Dr John Watson, a recently discharged army Medic who is looking to shift his luck around in london by finding a flatmate. Within 24 hours, the two move into 221B Baker street
  • March 4th, 1881 - Sherlock introduces Dr John Watson to his working methods
  • Mach 20th-22nd, 1888 - During Sherlock's case "A Study in Scarlet" he encounters Irene Adler for the first time. From this moment on Sherlock refers to Irene as "The woman" as she transcended and "eclispsed" the rest of her sex.
  • July, 1888 - The first Sherlock Holmes book "A Study in Scarlet" by Arthur Conan Doyle is Published (originally published as a story in a magazine called The Beeton's Christmas Annual in 1887)
  • 1957 - Though Sherlock's death remains the largest mystery of his life, it has been surmised by famous Sherlockian William Baring-Gould that Sherlock died on his 103rd birthday in 1957.
"How often have I said to you that when you have eliminated the impossible, whatever remains, however improbable, must be the truth?" - Sherlock Holmes
